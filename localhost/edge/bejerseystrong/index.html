Far too often, people find that they lack health care
just when they need it.

Whether a young adult or a new American, living without health insurance means that every day is a gamble. Catastrophic medical expenses and bankruptcy are always just an injury or accident away.

Most uninsured individuals know this. They know how precarious it is to be uninsured and want health insurance. They are, however, simply overwhelmed by the health care marketplace. What they need are helping hands and one-on-one conversations to help them make a well-informed decision about their health insurance options.

Over 250,000 New Jerseyans are uninsured.

The majority of them want insurance, but over 50% feel overwhelmed by the process.

Knowing that low-cost plans, financial support, and in-person help are available can mean the difference between enrolling and not enrolling.

That's where Be Jersey Strong comes in to play.

In September 2015, one thousand college students will form a grassroots movement to help New Jerseyans learn about their health insurance options.

Be Jersey Strong will work with regional coordinators to identify and train campus captains and volunteers at each two- and four-year college in New Jersey.

Campus captains and volunteers will then speak at churches, community centers, clinics, and local events to connect individuals to the information and resources they need to make a well-informed decision about their health insurance options.

Together, they will aim to mobilize over 1,000 volunteers and have over 100,000 conversations with their fellow New Jerseyans.
